The history of DDoS (Distributed Denial of Service) attacks traces back to the early days of the internet, where the concept emerged as a means of disrupting online services by flooding target systems with an overwhelming volume of traffic. One of the earliest recorded instances dates back to 1999 when the now-infamous "MafiaBoy" launched a series of DDoS attacks against major e-commerce sites, including Yahoo!, eBay, and Amazon. These attacks highlighted the vulnerability of centralized internet infrastructure and spurred efforts to develop countermeasures. Over the years, DDoS attacks have evolved in sophistication and scale, fueled by the proliferation of botnets and advancements in networking technology. From politically motivated disruptions to extortion attempts and ideological clashes, DDoS attacks have become a prevalent tool for cybercriminals and hacktivists alike, underscoring the critical importance of robust cybersecurity measures in today's interconnected digital landscape.
